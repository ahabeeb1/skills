# Plan: v1.22.0 Methodology Overhaul

## TL;DR

We're shipping five interlocking methodology changes plus one deferred-hardening ADR. Plans become readable to a cold reader. The first human review gate in `/research` moves from "after the spec is drafted" to "right after the recommendation is synthesized" — saving downstream token spend when a pivot is needed. A new advisory hook warns when chain artifacts drift out of sync. ADRs and plans gain four PascalCase frontmatter fields so the release skill can flag stale decisions. And we acknowledge that habeebs-skill ships shell hooks with no upstream threat model, naming three concrete research targets for v1.23.0+. This very plan is the first artifact written in the new plain-English plan format — if it's hard to read, the format is broken.

## Goal

Every habeebs-skill plan written from v1.22.0 onward is readable cold by a human who has never seen the methodology before, every `/research` run halts for a human pivot decision before paying for downstream artifacts, and every hook and ADR carries enough metadata to surface drift at release time.

## Success measure

After v1.22.0 ships and the next minor release (v1.23.0 or v1.22.1 patch) is cut, the release skill's editorial scan runs against the v1.22.0 plan + ADRs without producing dormancy warnings, and the next `/research` invocation halts for the HITL pivot exactly as documented in `prior-art-research/SKILL.md`. The all-13-dogfood-scenarios-plus-the-new-29 baseline passes throughout.

## Phases

### Phase 1 — Foundation (AFK parallel)

This phase ships the documentation-only deliverables that have no chain-handoff coupling. The two ADRs from this release (the methodology bundle and the supply-chain deferred-hardening) are already on disk from `/record`; this phase verifies them and adds the two prose paragraphs to CLAUDE.md and AGENTS.md that codify the runtime-writer-path-versus-authored-methodology distinction from ADR-0021's 2026-05-26 Clarification. Both slices in this phase are AFK and disjoint — they can run in parallel via `parallel-dev` write-task dispatch in their own sub-worktrees. *AFK means autonomous-friendly; the slice can be implemented and merged without human gating mid-slice. `parallel-dev` is habeebs-skill's primitive for dispatching independent AFK slices concurrently, each in its own git worktree.*

**Acceptance gate.** The phase is done when all four of these are simultaneously true: (1) the two new ADRs at `docs/agents/adrs/adr-methodology-bundle-v1.22.md` and `docs/agents/adrs/adr-plugin-supply-chain-threat-model.md` each contain Status: Accepted, the four PascalCase telemetry fields, at least three alternatives, at least three revisit triggers, and cross-links to the relevant prior ADRs; (2) CLAUDE.md § "Agent skills" inventory and AGENTS.md inventory each carry one prose paragraph explicitly marking `dispatches/` and `conflicts/` as runtime writer paths (not dormant directories); (3) all 28 cross-references to `docs/agents/dispatches/` or `docs/agents/conflicts/` across the 10 active-surface files (skill bodies, dogfood scenarios, audit tests) verifiably point at load-bearing writer-path documentation, not dead links; (4) all 13 dogfood scenarios (5 baselines plus 21 through 28) pass.

**Top risks.** The biggest risk is that one of the 28 cross-references turns out to be a dead link rather than load-bearing — the grill found all 28 ARE load-bearing, but a tracer cleanup in Slice 1 still verifies this empirically. Mitigation: Slice 1's verification step lists every hit; if any one fails the "points at writer-path contract" test, halt and re-scope. The second risk is documentation drift between CLAUDE.md and AGENTS.md — the two files mirror each other and divergent paragraphs would surface later as inconsistency. Mitigation: write one paragraph and apply to both files in the same commit. The third risk is that the two ADRs from `/record` use the new PascalCase frontmatter convention before Slice 4 formally codifies the convention in templates — readers may see the new fields and not know they're load-bearing until Slice 4 runs. Mitigation: dogfooding from day 1 is the intentional design (per Concrete pick #11 in the spec), and Slice 4 retroactively documents what these ADRs already demonstrate.

**Rollback hook.** Single `git revert` per commit reverses the prose-paragraph additions cleanly. The two ADRs from `/record` cannot be cleanly "unadded" once the late-binding rename runs at release, but the worst-case rollback is reverting their content to placeholder. This is not a one-way door.

### Phase 2 — Methodology rewrites (HITL)

This phase ships the four methodology changes that affect how the chain runs going forward. Slice 3 rewrites the plan format and extends GLOSSARY.md (it has to land first because two later slices touch the plan template too). Once Slice 3 lands, Slice 4 (telemetry frontmatter rollout) and Slice 5 (HITL pivot in `prior-art-research`) can run in parallel because they touch disjoint skills. Slice 6 (the PostToolUse validator hook) lands last because its warning text must align with the HITL-pivot phrasing from Slice 5. Every slice in this phase is HITL — *HITL:approval-gate means Modie reviews each commit out-of-band (chat session) before it lands.* The plan-format and HITL-pivot rewrites are meta-load-bearing — the words used in the SKILL.md text become the prompt every future chain run reads.

**Acceptance gate.** The phase is done when all six of these are simultaneously true: (1) `skills/write-plan/SKILL.md` body and `skills/write-plan/references/plan-template.md` enforce the new plain-English format (TL;DR top-of-doc, per-phase narrative intro, tables only for status block and slice list, numbered prose acceptance gates, prose-paragraph risk register); (2) GLOSSARY.md has seven new entries (`pgroup`, `pre-flight verification`, `HITL:approval-gate`, `HITL:per-file`, `HITL:inline`, `AFK:full-auto`, `tracer slice`); (3) `skills/decision-record/SKILL.md`, `skills/decision-record/references/adr-template.md`, `skills/write-plan/references/plan-template.md` status block, and `skills/release/SKILL.md` Phase 10 all instruct writers/scanners on the four PascalCase telemetry fields with deliberate-review semantics on `Last-Reviewed` and minor+major-only scan cadence; (4) `skills/prior-art-research/SKILL.md` has the new "Phase 6 ready for HITL review — pivot point" gate text between Phase 6 and Phase 6.5 with all three response options documented, and `skills/using-habeebs-skill/SKILL.md` mentions the new gate; (5) `hooks/check-chain-state.sh` exists, is wired in `hooks/hooks.json` for PostToolUse[Edit|Write|NotebookEdit] with timeout: 10, warns on the two scopes from grill OQ-1 (missing-grill-when-Grilled and editing-on-default-no-worktree), exits 0 always, honors `HABEEBS_DISABLE_HOOKS=1`; (6) new dogfood scenario 29 at `tests/dogfood/29-chain-state-validator/check-validator.sh` covers four fixture cases and passes alongside the existing 13.

**Top risks.** The biggest risk is plan-format adoption stalls because the rewritten template is too prescriptive or too loose — Modie is the sole reader-and-writer, so if THIS plan is hard for him to read, Slice 3's template is wrong. Mitigation: this plan IS the live test; if Modie pushes back on any section during plan review, Slice 3 adjusts the template before landing. The second risk is the PostToolUse validator hook produces enough false positives that Modie disables it within the first week (the warn-fatigue scenario flagged by grill OQ-1). Mitigation: scope is locked tight in grill (only two warning conditions, both rejected variants explicitly justified); revisit trigger fires if Modie ignores ≥3 warnings in a row without acting. The third risk is the HITL pivot text in `prior-art-research/SKILL.md` reads differently from what we actually dogfooded on this run, creating a meta-inconsistency that future readers can't reconcile. Mitigation: Slice 5's acceptance test is to re-read this run's chain transcript and confirm the gate behavior matches; if it doesn't, the SKILL.md text gets reworded.

**Rollback hook.** Each slice in this phase is one (or a small number of) commits; `git revert <slice-commit>` undoes any specific slice without affecting others. Per-slice granularity provides surgical rollback. Slice 6 has one additional rollback path: setting `HABEEBS_DISABLE_HOOKS=1` in the environment turns off the validator hook without code revert.

### Phase 3 — Self-dogfood release (HITL terminal)

This phase consumes the v1.20.0 changeset mechanism on the v1.22.0 release itself, the same pattern v1.20.0 and v1.21.0 followed. *Self-dogfood means we release using the release-mechanism we built — every methodology change must survive its own release ceremony before shipping.* Slice 7 creates the changeset, runs the aggregator and ADR-ID-assignment scripts, opens the PR, and pushes the tag after Modie merges. The two new ADRs get renamed from `adr-<slug>.md` to `NNNN-<slug>.md` (0023 + 0024 in alphabetic slug order) by the late-binding mechanism per ADR-0020.

**Acceptance gate.** The phase is done when all five of these are simultaneously true: (1) `.changeset/v1.22.0-methodology-overhaul.md` is created with `bump: minor` and a one-line `why:` summary, then consumed and deleted by the aggregator; (2) `.claude-plugin/plugin.json` and `.claude-plugin/marketplace.json` both show `version: 1.22.0` and `CHANGELOG.md` has a new `## [1.22.0]` section with the bundled changes summarized; (3) the two new ADRs are renamed to `0023-methodology-bundle-v1.22.md` and `0024-plugin-supply-chain-threat-model.md` (alphabetic slug order: 'm' before 'p'), and `docs/agents/adrs/README.md` is regenerated to include them; (4) all 14 dogfood scenarios pass (5 baselines plus 21 through 28 plus the new 29); (5) the PR opens, Modie merges, and the `v1.22.0` tag pushes to origin with a GitHub release published.

**Top risks.** The biggest risk is the aggregator script hits a regex edge case it didn't hit in v1.20.0 or v1.21.0 — every previous release has surfaced exactly one new bug in this script (this is the third self-dogfood pass). Mitigation: dry-run first (`aggregate-changesets.sh --dry-run` and `assign-adr-ids.sh --dry-run`); if any dry-run produces unexpected output, halt and fix before live run. The second risk is late-binding slug collision — both new ADRs start with "adr-" so the rename order matters. Mitigation: only two ADRs added in v1.22.0, alphabetic slug order is deterministic (`adr-methodology-bundle-v1.22` precedes `adr-plugin-supply-chain-threat-model`), no slug ties. The third risk is dogfood scenario 29 from Slice 6 mis-fires on the release commit itself (the validator hook scans skill changes during the release PR's own edits). Mitigation: validator is warn-only — even a mis-fire prints to stderr and exits 0; the release continues uninterrupted.

**Rollback hook.** Pre-tag, the changeset is unaggregated until the aggregator runs — no rollback needed because nothing has changed. The aggregator script's atomic-or-rollback semantics (proven across v1.20.0 and v1.21.0 dogfood scenario 23) keep the working tree clean on write failure. Post-tag, the rollback is `git tag -d v1.22.0` locally plus `git push origin :refs/tags/v1.22.0` to delete remote, then revert the PR merge. This is recoverable but not free; the release should not be tagged unless all 14 scenarios pass.

## Risk register

The risks are enumerated per-phase in the phase narratives above with embedded "Mitigation:" prose. The three highest-impact cross-phase risks are summarized here:

1. **Plan-format adoption stalls.** Slice 3's rewrite produces a template that's either too prescriptive (Modie pushes back on every plan) or too loose (no convergent shape; future plans drift). Likelihood Medium, Impact Medium. Mitigation: this very plan is the live test of the template; iterate the template until this plan reads cleanly to Modie cold.
2. **HITL pivot drop-out rate is too high.** If 50%+ of future `/research` runs hit the new Phase 6 gate and the user accepts as-is unchanged, the gate is overhead. Likelihood Medium, Impact Low (demote to opt-in via `--pivot` flag in v1.23.0+ — non-breaking). Mitigation: measure across the first three `/research` runs after v1.22.0 ships; if zero of three required a pivot, revisit triggers fire and the gate becomes opt-in.
3. **PostToolUse validator warn-fatigue.** Modie ignores warnings; the hook becomes background noise. Likelihood Medium, Impact Low (warn-only; never blocks). Mitigation: revisit trigger fires after three consecutive ignored warnings; scope-narrows in v1.23.0+ or demotes to opt-in.

The release-aggregator regex regression risk is captured in Phase 3's narrative. The cross-reference-cleanup risk is captured in Phase 1's narrative.

## Revisit triggers

This plan should be reopened — and `socratic-grill` re-run on the affected section — if any of:

- Plan-format adoption stalls (per Risk 1 above): 3+ contributors find the new format opaque, OR Modie reads three consecutive new plans and explicitly says "this is harder to use than the old shape."
- HITL pivot drop-out rate (per Risk 2 above): 50%+ of `/research` runs accept-as-is without modification across the first six runs after v1.22.0 ships.
- PostToolUse validator warn-fatigue (per Risk 3 above): three consecutive warnings ignored without action.
- Telemetry back-fill becomes load-bearing: 5+ existing ADRs go > 6 months without `Date:` updates AND the new `Last-Reviewed` field starts showing dormancy → ship the v1.23.0+ back-fill slice for the 22 retroactive ADRs.
- Plugin supply-chain real incident: the deferred-hardening ADR's revisit triggers fire (Anthropic ships signature primitive, OR Claude Code marketplace hits documented compromise, OR habeebs-skill exceeds 100 installs/month) — promote Piece 6 deferrals to v1.23.0+ research.
- ADR-0021 re-re-re-amendment: any future ADR declares a NEW empty methodology directory → the writer-path-vs-authored-methodology lesson didn't take; deeper governance change required.
- Dogfood scenario 26/27/28 (from v1.21.0) OR new scenario 29 produces > 1 false positive per release → re-evaluate the detection regex or hook scope.
