## Trade-offs accepted

From research § 3 and § 4:

- **Methodology shrinks rather than grows.** Removes 2 empty directories, ~50% of plan-template structure, late-stage HITL gates. The chain becomes thinner.
- **Warn-only hook accepts some warn-fatigue risk.** Mitigated by tight scope (locked in `/grill` per OQ-1). Modie can ignore warnings; the cost is signal, not block.
- **Telemetry deferral leaves 22 existing ADRs without `last-reviewed`.** Editorial scans at release time only catch new ADRs in `Proposed`/`Active` status. Existing ADRs' staleness remains invisible to the scan until back-fill ships.
- **Piece 6 supply-chain ADR is honest about not solving the problem.** Names the gap, defers signature/sandboxing/data-marker hardening to v1.23.0+. Some readers will want action; the action is the named research targets, not crypto.
- **HITL pivot adds one round-trip to every research run.** Cost: one halt + one user message per `/research` invocation. Benefit: prevents wasted spec/grill/record/plan tokens when the recommendation needs to pivot.
- **Plan-format rewrite breaks template-consistency with v1.21.0 and earlier plans.** No migration of existing plans (they stay in old shape); only NEW plans use the new template. Backstage's "manual reclassification" pattern (SP5) suggests this is the right level of friction.
- **Cross-reference cleanup (OQ-5) may surface more references than expected.** Mitigated by Slice 1's grep step doubling as discovery; if references > 20, halt and re-scope.
